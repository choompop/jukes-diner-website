## CONTEXT SUMMARY

This plan distills the brewery segment from the broader 4-part booking outreach playbook (breweries, schools, businesses, apartments) built in task t_9fd86412. Breweries are prioritized as a proven food truck booking channel with:
- Strong weekend foot traffic
- Community-oriented brand fit
- Established food truck partnership culture in Austin
- Low operational friction (outdoor setup, existing customer base)

**Relevant Juke's Context:**
- Nashville hot chicken menu (tenders, sandwiches, sides)
- Self-contained truck (no host kitchen needed)
- Austin-based with social media presence
- Looking for recurring weekend spots and one-off events
- No established brewery relationships on record (greenfield opportunity)

## DRAFT OUTREACH MESSAGE

**Channel:** Email (primary) → Instagram DM (7-day follow-up if no response)

**Subject Line:**  
Juke's Diner x [BREWERY NAME] — Weekend Food Truck Partnership

**Message Body:**

---

Hi [CONTACT NAME / Team],

I'm reaching out from Juke's Diner, a Nashville hot chicken food truck based in Austin. We're booking spring/summer partnerships with local breweries and think [BREWERY NAME] would be a great fit for your weekend crowd.

What we bring:
- Classic Nashville hot chicken (tenders, sandwiches, sides) — comfort food that pairs well with craft beer
- Self-contained setup — we handle all service, no kitchen needed
- Social media promotion to our followers
- Flexible scheduling (weekends, recurring slots, or one-off events)

We'd love to start with a trial weekend to see how it goes. Are you the right person to discuss food truck partnerships, or could you point me to your events coordinator?

Looking forward to connecting!

Best,  
[SIGNATURE — John or Juke's Team]

---

**Tone Notes:**
- Concise, partnership-focused (not transactional)
- Emphasizes value to brewery (foot traffic draw, no operational lift)
- Low-commitment ask (trial weekend)
- Open loop for scheduling details

## PRICING STRATEGY (NEEDS CONFIRMATION)

**Critical blocker:** Pricing model not confirmed in available context.

**Options to clarify with John:**
1. **Revenue share** (e.g., 10-15% of sales to brewery) — common model
2. **Flat fee** (brewery pays fixed rate for truck presence)
3. **Free for brewery** (Juke's keeps all sales, brewery gets foot traffic/amenity)
4. **Hybrid** (small fee + revenue share)

**Next step:** Block or clarify pricing before high-volume outreach to avoid inconsistent offers.

## RISKS & MITIGATION

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Low response rate | Start with warm intro if possible (mutual connections, prior event attendance) |
| Pricing misalignment | Finalize pricing tiers before sending (see above) |
| Over-commitment | Cap at 2-3 recurring weekend spots initially to test operational capacity |
| Brand mismatch | Prioritize breweries with existing food truck culture (see target list notes) |
