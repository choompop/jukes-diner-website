## Voice Guidelines

### Core Principles
- **Warm, useful, and a little diner-counter charming**
- Short sentences. Clear next steps. No corporate fog.
- Sound like Flo: upbeat operator, not generic chatbot.

### Voice Character: Flo
Think of the classic diner server — friendly, efficient, knows the menu cold, remembers regulars, keeps things moving. Not cutesy, not overly casual, not corporate. Just reliably warm and competent.

### Do's
✅ "Tell us your date, headcount, and location — we'll send you a quote."  
✅ "Book Juke's for your next event."  
✅ "We'll follow up within 24 hours."  
✅ "Smashed patties, hand-spun shakes, service with a smile."

### Don'ts
❌ Generic corporate speak: "We're excited to partner with you on this journey"  
❌ Overly precious copy: "Our artisanal, farm-to-table culinary experience"  
❌ Chatbot tone: "I'd be happy to help you with that today!"  
❌ Vague promises: "We'll get back to you soon"

### Copy Rules by Context

**Public copy (website, social, ads):**  
→ Create appetite, trust, and booking intent  
→ Lead with food quality and service reliability  
→ Always include clear next step (CTA)

**Internal copy (dashboard, ops docs, team communications):**  
→ Create clarity, ownership, and follow-through  
→ Name who, what, when explicitly  
→ Flag urgency and escalation paths

**Customer communications (quotes, confirmations, follow-ups):**  
→ Confirm details up front  
→ Set clear expectations (timing, pricing, deliverables)  
→ Warm but professional — build trust for repeat business

## Visual Design Language

### Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Diner Red** | `#d62828` | Primary CTAs, hero accents, urgent flags |
| **Cream** | `#f8f1e5` | Page backgrounds, card backgrounds, secondary text |
| **Teal** | `#2a9d8f` | Secondary CTAs, feature highlights, success states |
| **Black** | `#1a1a1a` | Body text, header backgrounds, borders |
| **Chrome** | `#e5e5e5` | Accent borders, subtle dividers, shine effects |

### Color Usage Rules
- **Backgrounds:** Cream (primary), Black (dramatic sections)
- **Text:** Black on Cream, Cream on Black
- **CTAs:** Red (primary action), Teal (secondary action), Black with Cream border (tertiary)
- **Alerts/Warnings:** Red text or red border
- **Success states:** Teal
- **Never:** Red text on Black, Teal text on Black (readability issues)

### Typography

**Font Stack:**
- **Display (headings, CTAs):** Bungee — bold, retro, high impact
- **Body:** Inter — clean, readable, modern
- **Monospace (data, code):** JetBrains Mono
- **Serif (accent quotes, testimonials):** Playfair Display

**Heading hierarchy:**
- H1: Font-display, tight tracking, bold weight
- H2-H6: Font-display, tight tracking
- Body: Font-sans, normal weight
- Captions: Font-sans, smaller size, medium weight

**Rules:**
- Keep headings short and punchy (classic diner sign style)
- Use sentence case, not TITLE CASE (except for intentional retro all-caps accents)
- Avoid italics in headings (use it sparingly in body for emphasis only)

### Visual Motifs & Patterns

**Approved motifs:**
- ✅ Checkerboard patterns (low opacity background accents)
- ✅ Ticket/order slip metaphors (forms, receipts, confirmations)
- ✅ Booth/table/counter language (UI sections as "booths")
- ✅ Chrome/metallic shine accents (borders, dividers)
- ✅ Neon glow effects (text-shadow on hero elements, sparingly)

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Food photography clichés (extreme close-ups, stock food shots)
- ❌ Overly literal 1950s pastiche (poodle skirts, jukeboxes everywhere)
- ❌ Busy textures that reduce readability
- ❌ Gradients outside of subtle shine/glow effects

## Dashboard-Specific Guidance

### Dashboard Target
`dashboard.jukesdiner.com` — internal ops tool for John, team leads, and Flo agent workflows.

### Dashboard Design Principles
1. **Clarity over charm** — function first, brand accents second
2. **Scannable data** — use tables, cards, clear headings
3. **Action-oriented** — every screen should have a clear next step
4. **Status visibility** — use color-coded badges for order status, lead status, etc.

### Dashboard Component Standards
**Navigation:**
- Clean sidebar or top nav
- Active state clearly indicated (red or teal highlight)

**Data display:**
- Monospace fonts for numbers, dates, currency
- Consistent date format (YYYY-MM-DD or Month DD, YYYY)
- Color-coded status badges (red = urgent, teal = complete, grey = pending)

**Forms:**
- Required fields marked clearly
- Validation errors in red with specific messaging
- Success confirmations in teal

**Cards:**
- Use glass-card effect for elevated content
- Consistent padding and spacing
- Clear headings with font-display

## Quality Standards

### Copy Review Checklist
Before publishing any copy:
- [ ] Voice sounds like Flo (warm, clear, useful)
- [ ] Next step is explicit (CTA, follow-up action)
- [ ] No vague promises or corporate fog
- [ ] Facts are sourced (menu, pricing, availability)
- [ ] Tone matches context (public vs. internal vs. customer)

### Design Review Checklist
Before implementing designs:
- [ ] Color contrast meets accessibility standards
- [ ] Typography hierarchy is clear
- [ ] Retro accents enhance, don't overwhelm
- [ ] Mobile-responsive layouts work
- [ ] Loading states and error states designed
