## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This plan extracts and expands the apartment complex segment from the 4-part booking outreach playbook (task t_9fd86412). Apartment complexes represent a high-value booking channel with:
- **Recurring revenue potential** (monthly resident events, move-in season activations)
- **Sponsor-paid model** (property pays, residents eat free → low friction conversion)
- **Multi-property leverage** (property management companies control 10-50+ Austin complexes)
- **Lower competition** than breweries/festivals (fewer food trucks target resident amenities)
- **Scalable calendar fill** (weeknight events, not just weekend-dependent)

**Juke's Context:**
- Nashville hot chicken menu (tenders, sandwiches, sides) — comfort food popular with young professionals
- Self-contained truck (no complex kitchen needed, fits parking lot setups)
- Austin-based with social media presence (can tag property + create resident content)
- Looking for weeknight/weekend fill, especially non-peak times
- No established apartment relationships on record (greenfield opportunity)

## PRICING STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS

**Two models to offer (John to finalize numbers):**

### Model 1: Sponsor-Paid (Property Pays, Residents Eat Free)
- **Flat event fee** — $500-800 for 2-hour event (estimated 50-100 servings)
- **Positioning:** "Turn-key resident appreciation — we handle everything, you get the credit"
- **Best for:** Luxury properties, management companies with amenity budgets
- **Upsell:** Monthly recurring contract (e.g., first Friday of every month)

### Model 2: Resident-Paid (Discounted Pricing, Property Promotes)
- **Discounted resident pricing** — 10-15% off regular menu pricing
- **Property fee:** $0-150 (minimal or waived to reduce property friction)
- **Positioning:** "Zero-cost amenity for your residents, we share revenue with property"
- **Best for:** Value-conscious properties, suburban complexes

### Recommended Starter Offer
**Trial Event Discount:** First event at 20% off to reduce property's perceived risk and build proof of concept.

## OBJECTION HANDLING GUIDE

**Common objections & suggested responses (John to refine/approve):**

### "We already have a regular food truck vendor"
**Response:** "That's great! Are you looking for variety or backup options for special events? We're happy to rotate in for monthly activations or move-in season."

### "We don't have budget for resident events right now"
**Response:** "Totally understand. We also offer a resident-paid model where your residents get discounted pricing and the property incurs little to no cost. Would that work better?"

### "We need to see a proposal first"
**Response:** "Happy to send one over. Can I ask — what's the typical format for your resident events? (e.g., happy hour, weekend brunch, pool party) I'll tailor the proposal to fit."

### "I'll need to discuss with my manager/corporate"
**Response:** "Of course! Would it help if I sent you a one-pager you can forward to them? Or would you prefer I connect directly with [manager/corporate contact]?"

### "We're not planning any events in the next few months"
**Response:** "No problem! When do you typically start planning for [season]? I'm happy to reconnect then so we're on your radar."

## RISKS & MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Low Response Rate (<20%)
**Mitigation:** Switch to phone-first outreach (call → email) for individual properties. Property managers often respond better to calls.

### Risk 2: No Trial-to-Recurring Conversion
**Mitigation:** Offer discounted 3-event package ($500/event instead of $600) to lock in recurring commitment upfront.

### Risk 3: Pricing Too High for Budget-Conscious Properties
**Mitigation:** Promote resident-paid model as zero-cost amenity. Offer variable pricing tiers (basic menu vs. premium add-ons).

### Risk 4: Seasonal Timing (Slow Event Season)
**Mitigation:** Lead with "move-in season" (August-September) and "holiday appreciation" (November-December) as near-term hooks.
