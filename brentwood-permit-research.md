## Executive Summary

**YES**, Juke's Diner can legally serve in Brentwood, Tennessee as a mobile food vendor (food truck), subject to obtaining proper permits and following local regulations.

**Key Requirements:**
- City of Brentwood Mobile Food Vendor Permit (annual, $50-$100)
- Tennessee state health department license for mobile food units
- Written property owner permission for private property operations
- Compliance with zoning, operating hours, and safety regulations

---

## Regulatory Framework

### 1. City of Brentwood Ordinances

Brentwood has established comprehensive mobile food vendor regulations through two ordinances:

- **Ordinance 2017-01**: Primary regulations for mobile food vendors (Chapter 18, Article VI)
- **Ordinance 2017-02**: Zoning amendments allowing mobile food vending in various districts

These ordinances are current and govern all mobile food operations within Brentwood city limits.

### 2. Tennessee State Requirements

Mobile food units must also comply with Tennessee Department of Health regulations, including:
- State health department license/permit for mobile food providers
- Commissary requirements for food preparation and vehicle cleaning
- Food safety and sanitation standards

## Can Juke's Serve in Brentwood?

**YES - with the following conditions:**

### Where Juke's CAN Operate:

1. **Private Property** (most common scenario)
   - On improved commercial properties (with building/structure present)
   - With written permission from property owner
   - In zones where mobile food vending is permitted
   - Maximum 2 food trucks per location (unless food truck rally permit obtained)
   - Cannot operate on unimproved vacant lots

2. **Public Right-of-Way** (limited)
   - Generally NOT allowed except for special city-approved events
   - Ice cream trucks have more flexibility in right-of-way

3. **City Parks**
   - Subject to Park Board rules and approval

### Where Juke's CANNOT Operate:

- Public right-of-way without city permission
- Unimproved/vacant private property (must have a building)
- Locations blocking business access, fire lanes, or traffic
- Within 3 feet of another mobile food vendor

## Permit Process: Step-by-Step Guide

### STEP 1: Obtain Tennessee State Health Permit
**Timeline:** Allow 2-4 weeks  
**Responsible Party:** Operations Manager / Owner  
**Cost:** Contact Williamson County Health Department for fee schedule

**Requirements:**
- Tennessee Department of Health mobile food unit license
- Health inspection of food truck
- Proof of commissary (commercial kitchen for prep/cleaning)
- Food handler certifications for staff

**Contact:**
- Williamson County Health Department
- Website: https://www.tn.gov/health (search for Environmental Health/Food Safety)
- May also need to contact local health department office

### STEP 2: Obtain Business License
**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks  
**Responsible Party:** Owner  
**Cost:** Standard business license fee

**Requirements:**
- Business registration with Tennessee Secretary of State
- Local business tax license

### STEP 3: Obtain Insurance
**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks  
**Responsible Party:** Owner / Operations Manager  
**Cost:** Varies by carrier and coverage

**Requirements:**
- Motor vehicle insurance (state-required minimums)
- General liability insurance
- Additional insurance if operating on city property (as required by City Manager)
- Certificate of insurance must be provided with permit application

### STEP 4: Apply for Brentwood Mobile Food Vendor Permit
**Timeline:** 15 days processing (per ordinance)  
**Responsible Party:** Operations Manager  
**Cost:** 
- **$50** - First-time or renewal application
- **$100** - If previously found operating without permit

**Application Requirements:**
- Name and address of vehicle owner
- Name and address of operator
- Color photographs of vehicle (front, side, back) showing all markings
- Copy of vehicle license and registration with VIN
- Copy of Tennessee health department license/permit
- Copy of business license
- Certificate of insurance
- Signed acknowledgement of ordinance compliance
- Any additional information required by permit administrator

**Submit to:**
- Planning and Codes Department
- City of Brentwood
- 5211 Maryland Way, Brentwood, TN 37027
- Phone: (615) 371-0060
- Email: info@brentwoodtn.gov

**Permit Details:**
- Valid through December 31 each year
- Renewal required annually (same $50 fee)
- Permit must be prominently displayed when operating
- Cannot be transferred (except with business sale)
- Can be suspended/revoked for violations

### STEP 5: Secure Property Owner Permission
**Timeline:** Ongoing as needed  
**Responsible Party:** Sales / Operations Manager  
**Cost:** May involve location fees negotiated with property owner

**Requirements:**
- Written permission from property owner
- Must be available to city immediately upon request
- Should specify operating days/times agreed upon

### STEP 6 (If Applicable): Food Truck Rally Permit
**Timeline:** 30 days minimum before event  
**Responsible Party:** Event organizer (may be property owner)  
**Cost:** 
- $50 for smaller rallies
- Planning Commission fee for rallies with 10+ trucks

**When Required:**
- More than 2 mobile food trucks at one location
- Coordinated, advertised gathering on a specific date

## Operating Requirements Summary

### Hours of Operation
- **Food Trucks:** 7:00 AM to 9:00 PM (unless restricted by property owner or permit)
- Extended hours possible for approved food truck rallies

### Location Restrictions
- Maximum 4 parking spaces per food truck (including canopies, seating, equipment)
- Cannot block fire lanes, traffic lanes, or building access
- Must maintain 6-foot pedestrian pathway if near sidewalks
- Minimum 3 feet from other mobile food vendors

### Operational Compliance
- **Pedestrian service only** (no drive-through service)
- Proper trash receptacles required
- Area within 50 feet of truck must be kept clean
- All waste removed before departure
- Fire extinguishers (NFPA certified, inspected annually)
- Spill response plan required
- No stakes/rods driven into pavement

### Signage
- Signs on vehicle exterior/interior allowed
- One sandwich board sign allowed (max 8 sq ft per side, 48" height)
- Sandwich board must be within 2 feet of vehicle

### Alcohol Sales
- Generally prohibited for food trucks
- Only allowed if specifically permitted by state law and city ordinance
